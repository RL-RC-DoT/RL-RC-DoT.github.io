RL-RC-DoT: A Block-level RL agent for Task-Aware Video Compression

RL-RC-DoT: A Block-level RL agent for Task-Aware Video Compression

Uri Gadot1,2, Assaf Shocher2, Shie Mannor1,2, Gal Chechik2, Assaf Hallak2
1Technion, 2NVIDIA,

CVPR 2025

arXiv

RL-RC-DoT improves the quality of the downstream task under the same bit-rate constraints.

Abstract

Video encoders traditionally optimize for human perception, but in applications like autonomous driving, videos primarily serve AI systems. We propose optimizing encoders for downstream tasks by controlling Quantization Parameters at the macro-block level, prioritizing task-relevant regions. Using Reinforcement Learning, our approach balances task performance with bit-rate constraints without requiring downstream task input during inference. This makes it suitable for streaming and edge devices. We demonstrate significant improvements in car detection and ROI encoding compared to traditional methods, enabling more efficient task-aware video compression.

What is RL-RC-DoT?

We present RL-RC-DoT, an RL-based Rate Controller for Downstream Task, that dynamically optimizes per-block QP deltas during video encoding. Our approach trains a deep-RL agent that allocates bandwidth to different patches in each frame, only observing fast-to-compute features of every frame.

Our proposed solution to the block-level control for a downstream task. RL-RC-DoT takes encoder statistics as input and outputs a block-level delta QP map. We then evaluate the difference in downstream task performance between the reconstructed frame and the raw frame. The reward contains both global score as reward and block-level score.

Car detection downstream task

Rate-Distortion (RD) curves plot the trade-off between bit-rate and quality metrics, while BD-rate (Bjøntegaard Delta rate) measures the average bit-rate savings between two encoding methods at the same quality level, making it an industry standard for compression efficiency comparison.

RL-RC-DoT shows significant improvements in detection performance, with BD-rate reductions of 24.7% (±1.38%). These gains come at a minimal cost to overall video quality, yielding a slight increase in PSNR BD-rate of 1.19% (±0.46%).

QP-map analysis

We compare the QP map selection with Eigen-CAM spatial maps. RL-RC-DoT's QP map shows a higher correlation with the Eigen-CAM spatial maps, indicating that our approach effectively identifies and prioritizes task-critical regions in video frames. This alignment, confirmed by low KL-divergence values between QP maps and Eigen-CAM spatial maps, reveals that our method intuitively learns to allocate more bits to areas most informative for downstream tasks like car detection.



Task robustness

The robustness of RL-RC-DoT extended beyond its original training task. When tested on car segmentation—a different but related task—RL-RC-DoT continued to show improved performance. While the BD-rate improvement for segmentation was not as pronounced as for detection, this cross-task effectiveness suggests that the method captures fundamental aspects of visual information relevant to automotive perception tasks more broadly.
